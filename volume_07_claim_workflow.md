# 7.1 What is a Claim Workflow?

A claim workflow is the sequence of steps followed by an insurance company to evaluate and settle a health insurance claim.

The purpose of the workflow is to ensure that every claim is processed fairly, accurately, and according to the policy terms.

Typical stages include:

- Claim Submission
- Document Verification
- Medical Assessment
- Policy Validation
- Decision Making
- Claim Settlement

---

# 7.2 Claim Submission

The claim process begins when the insured person or hospital submits a claim.

The submission should include all mandatory documents.

Typical documents include:

- Claim Form
- Hospital Bill
- Itemized Bill
- Discharge Summary
- Doctor's Prescription
- Diagnostic Reports
- Identity Proof
- Policy Details

Incomplete submissions may delay claim processing.

Example:

A claimant submits all required documents online.

Result:

The insurer accepts the claim for further assessment.

---

# 7.3 Document Verification

After a claim is submitted, the insurer verifies all supporting documents.

The verification process ensures that:

- All mandatory documents have been submitted.
- The documents are complete and readable.
- Patient details are consistent across all records.
- Hospital details are valid.
- Bill amounts match the itemized bill.
- Admission and discharge dates are correct.
- Medical reports support the diagnosis and treatment.

If any document is missing or inconsistent, additional clarification may be requested before proceeding.

Example:

The hospital bill and discharge summary contain different patient names.

Result:

The claim is placed on hold until the discrepancy is resolved.

---

# 7.4 Medical Assessment

During medical assessment, the insurer reviews the clinical details of the claim.

The assessment verifies:

- Diagnosis
- Treatment provided
- Medical necessity
- Hospitalization duration
- Whether the treatment is covered under the policy
- Whether any exclusion applies
- Whether waiting period conditions are satisfied

The insurer may consult a medical expert if additional review is required.

Example:

Diagnosis:
Acute Appendicitis

Treatment:
Appendectomy Surgery

Result:

The treatment is medically necessary and can proceed for policy validation.

---

# 7.5 Policy Validation

The insurer validates the policy before making a decision.

Checks include:

- Policy is active.
- Premium has been paid.
- Patient is an insured member.
- Sum insured is available.
- Waiting period requirements are completed.
- No policy exclusions apply.
- Coverage exists for the claimed treatment.

Example:

Policy Status:
Active

Premium:
Paid

Waiting Period:
Completed

Coverage:
Available

Result:

The claim qualifies for the final decision stage.

---

# 7.6 Decision Making

After document verification, medical assessment, and policy validation are completed, the insurer makes a final claim decision.

Possible decisions include:

- **Approve** – The claim satisfies all policy conditions and eligible expenses are payable.
- **Reject** – The claim does not satisfy one or more policy conditions.
- **Request More Information** – Additional documents or clarification are required before making a final decision.

The decision should be based on:

- Policy coverage
- Waiting periods
- Policy exclusions
- Available Sum Insured
- Fraud assessment
- Document verification

Example:

Policy Status:
Active

Coverage:
Available

Waiting Period:
Completed

Exclusions:
None

Fraud Indicators:
None

Result:

Claim Approved.

---

# 7.7 Claim Settlement

Once a claim is approved, the insurer settles the eligible amount according to the policy terms.

Settlement methods include:

### Cashless Settlement

- Payment is made directly to the network hospital.
- The insured pays only non-payable expenses, if any.

### Reimbursement Settlement

- The insured pays the hospital bill first.
- After verification, the insurer reimburses the eligible amount.

Settlement may consider:

- Co-payment
- Deductibles
- Room rent limits
- Non-medical expenses
- Available Sum Insured

Example:

Hospital Bill:
₹1,00,000

Eligible Amount:
₹92,000

Result:

The insurer settles ₹92,000 according to the policy conditions.
