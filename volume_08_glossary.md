# 8.1 Introduction

This glossary explains common health insurance terms used during claim adjudication.

It helps claim reviewers, healthcare professionals, and AI systems understand insurance terminology consistently.

---

# 8.2 Common Insurance Terms

### Premium

The amount paid by the policyholder to keep the health insurance policy active.

---

### Policyholder

The person who purchases the insurance policy.

---

### Insured Person

The individual covered under the health insurance policy.

---

### Health Insurance Policy

A legal contract between the insurer and the policyholder that defines coverage, exclusions, waiting periods, and claim conditions.

---

### Sum Insured

The maximum amount payable by the insurer during a policy year.

---

### Waiting Period

The period during which certain illnesses or treatments are not eligible for claim payment.

---

### Pre-existing Disease (PED)

A medical condition that existed before the policy was purchased.

---

### Coverage

Medical treatments and expenses that are payable according to the policy.

---

### Exclusion

A treatment, illness, or expense that is not covered under the policy.

---

### Co-payment (Co-pay)

The percentage of the eligible claim amount paid by the insured person.

---

### Deductible

The fixed amount the insured pays before insurance coverage begins.

---

### No Claim Bonus (NCB)

An additional benefit given to policyholders who do not make claims during a policy year.

---

# 8.3 Hospital Terms

### Admission Date

The date on which the patient is admitted to the hospital.

---

### Discharge Date

The date on which the patient leaves the hospital after treatment.

---

### Diagnosis

The medical condition identified by the treating doctor.

---

### Treatment

The medical procedure or care provided to the patient.

---

### Hospital Bill

The final invoice issued by the hospital for all medical services.

---

### Itemized Bill

A detailed breakdown of every charge included in the hospital bill.

---

### Discharge Summary

A document summarizing diagnosis, treatment, procedures, medications, and discharge instructions.

---

### ICU

Intensive Care Unit where critically ill patients receive specialized care.

---

### Day Care Procedure

A medical procedure that does not require 24-hour hospitalization.

---

# 8.4 Claim Processing Terms

### Cashless Claim

A claim where the insurer directly pays the eligible amount to the network hospital.

---

### Reimbursement Claim

A claim where the insured first pays the hospital bill and later receives reimbursement.

---

### Network Hospital

A hospital that has an agreement with the insurer or TPA to provide cashless treatment.

---

### Non-Network Hospital

A hospital that does not have a cashless arrangement with the insurer.

---

### Claim Approval

The insurer accepts the claim and agrees to pay eligible expenses.

---

### Claim Rejection

The insurer declines the claim because policy conditions are not satisfied.

---

### Request for More Information

The insurer requires additional documents or clarification before making a final decision.

---

# 8.5 Fraud & Investigation Terms

### Insurance Fraud

Intentional submission of false or misleading information to obtain insurance benefits.

---

### Duplicate Claim

Submitting the same medical expenses more than once for reimbursement.

---

### Altered Document

A medical or billing document that has been modified after it was issued.

---

### Identity Verification

The process of confirming that the claimant and insured person are the same individual.

---

### Fraud Investigation

A detailed review performed when suspicious claim activity is detected.
