# 2.1 Policy Eligibility

A health insurance policy can be purchased by an individual, a family, or a group. The insured person must satisfy the eligibility criteria defined by the insurer.

General eligibility rules include:

- The policyholder must provide accurate personal information.
- The premium must be paid on time.
- The policy must be active at the time of hospitalization.
- The insured person should not hide any important medical history during policy purchase.
- Any false declaration may lead to claim rejection.

Example:

Riya purchased a health insurance policy on 1 January 2025 and paid all premiums on time. She was hospitalized in March 2026. Since the policy was active, she was eligible to submit a claim.

---

# 2.2 Policy Activation

A health insurance policy becomes active only after:

- Premium payment is successfully received.
- The insurer issues the policy document.
- The policy start date has arrived.

A claim cannot be approved if:

- The policy has expired.
- Premium payment is overdue.
- The policy was cancelled before hospitalization.

Example:

Policy Start Date: 1 January 2025

Hospitalization Date: 10 February 2025

Result:
The policy is active and the claim can be processed.

Example:

Policy Expiry Date: 31 December 2025

Hospitalization Date: 15 January 2026

Result:
The policy expired before hospitalization. The claim is generally not payable.

# 2.3 Sum Insured

The Sum Insured is the maximum amount the insurance company will pay during a policy year.

Important points:

- It represents the maximum claim limit.
- Claims cannot exceed the available Sum Insured.
- Multiple claims during the year reduce the remaining Sum Insured.
- Once exhausted, additional claims may not be payable unless restoration benefits are available.

Example:

Sum Insured:
₹5,00,000

First Claim:
₹2,00,000

Remaining Sum Insured:
₹3,00,000

If another hospitalization costs ₹4,00,000, the insurer may pay only up to the available balance, subject to policy terms.

# 2.4 Room Rent Limits

Many health insurance policies specify a maximum room rent that can be claimed per day.

Common room rent limits include:

- 1% of the Sum Insured per day
- A fixed amount per day (for example, ₹5,000)
- No room rent limit for premium policies

If the insured chooses a room that exceeds the allowed limit, the insurer may proportionately reduce other hospital expenses as per the policy terms.

Example:

Room Rent Limit:
₹5,000 per day

Actual Room Rent:
₹7,000 per day

Result:
The claim may be settled according to the room rent eligibility defined in the policy.

# 2.5 ICU Charges

ICU (Intensive Care Unit) charges are generally covered when the patient's medical condition requires intensive care.

Coverage depends on:

- Medical necessity
- Policy terms and conditions
- Available Sum Insured

Premium plans may provide full ICU coverage, while some plans specify daily ICU limits.

Example:

ICU Stay:
3 days

ICU Charges:
₹45,000

If ICU treatment is medically necessary and covered by the policy, eligible expenses are payable according to policy terms.

# 2.6 Day Care Procedures

Some medical procedures do not require 24-hour hospitalization because of advancements in medical technology.

These are known as Day Care Procedures.

Common examples include:

- Cataract Surgery
- Dialysis
- Chemotherapy
- Radiotherapy
- Minor ENT surgeries
- Endoscopy
- Lithotripsy
- Angiography

If the procedure is listed as a covered day care treatment in the policy, hospitalization for 24 hours is generally not required.

Example:

A patient undergoes cataract surgery and is discharged on the same day.

Result:

The claim may still be eligible because cataract surgery is commonly covered as a day care procedure.

# 2.7 Network Hospitals

A Network Hospital is a hospital that has an agreement with the insurance company or Third Party Administrator (TPA).

Benefits of Network Hospitals:

- Cashless hospitalization facility.
- Faster claim processing.
- Less paperwork for the insured.
- Direct settlement between the insurer and hospital for eligible expenses.

If treatment is taken at a non-network hospital, the insured usually needs to pay the bill first and later apply for reimbursement.

Example:

Hospital:
ABC Multispeciality Hospital

Status:
Network Hospital

Result:
The patient may be eligible for cashless treatment, subject to policy approval.

---

# 2.8 Cashless Claims

In a cashless claim, the insurance company directly pays the eligible hospital expenses to the network hospital.

Requirements:

- Treatment must be taken at a network hospital.
- Valid health insurance policy.
- Pre-authorization approval from the insurer or TPA.
- Covered treatment under the policy.

Advantages:

- Reduced financial burden during hospitalization.
- Faster settlement.
- Minimal paperwork for the patient.

Example:

Hospital Bill:
₹1,20,000

Approved Claim:
₹1,00,000

The insurer pays ₹1,00,000 directly to the hospital. The patient pays only the remaining non-payable amount, if any.

---

# 2.9 Reimbursement Claims

A reimbursement claim is applicable when treatment is taken at a non-network hospital or when cashless approval is not available.

Process:

1. The patient pays the hospital bill.
2. The patient submits the claim documents.
3. The insurer verifies the documents.
4. Eligible expenses are reimbursed according to the policy.

Required Documents:

- Claim Form
- Hospital Bill
- Payment Receipts
- Discharge Summary
- Doctor's Prescription
- Diagnostic Reports

Example:

Hospital Bill:
₹80,000

Eligible Amount:
₹72,000

After verification, the insurer reimburses ₹72,000 to the insured person's bank account.

---

# 2.10 Co-payment

Co-payment (Co-pay) is the percentage of the claim amount that the insured person must pay from their own pocket.

The remaining eligible amount is paid by the insurance company.

Example:

Hospital Bill:
₹1,00,000

Co-pay:
10%

The insured pays:
₹10,000

The insurer pays:
₹90,000 (subject to policy terms).

---

# 2.11 Deductibles

A deductible is a fixed amount that the insured must pay before the insurance company starts paying eligible expenses.

Example:

Deductible:
₹20,000

Hospital Bill:
₹1,00,000

Eligible Claim:
₹80,000

The insured pays the first ₹20,000 and the insurer considers the remaining eligible amount according to the policy.

---

# 2.12 No Claim Bonus (NCB)

A No Claim Bonus is a reward given to policyholders who do not make any claims during a policy year.

Benefits may include:

- Increase in Sum Insured without increasing the premium.
- Discount on renewal premium (depending on the policy).
- Encourages healthy lifestyle and responsible claim behaviour.

Example:

Original Sum Insured:
₹5,00,000

No Claim Bonus:
20%

New Sum Insured:
₹6,00,000

---

# 2.13 Claim Approval Rules

A claim is generally approved when:

- The policy is active.
- The insured person is covered.
- The treatment is covered under the policy.
- Waiting period requirements are satisfied.
- No exclusion applies.
- Required documents are submitted.
- The claim amount is within the available Sum Insured.
- No fraud is detected.

Example:

If all policy conditions are satisfied, the insurer may approve the eligible claim amount.

---

# 2.14 Claim Rejection Rules

A claim may be rejected if:

- The policy is inactive or expired.
- Premium payment has not been made.
- The illness falls under policy exclusions.
- Waiting period has not been completed.
- Required documents are missing.
- Fraud or misrepresentation is detected.
- The treatment is not covered by the policy.
- The claim violates policy terms and conditions.

Example:

A customer purchases a policy and undergoes treatment for a disease that has a two-year waiting period after only three months.

Result:

The claim may be rejected because the waiting period has not been completed.
