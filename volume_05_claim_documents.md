# 5.1 Importance of Claim Documents

Health insurance claims are processed based on supporting documents submitted by the insured person.

These documents help the insurer:

- Verify the patient's identity.
- Confirm hospitalization.
- Validate the diagnosis and treatment.
- Calculate the eligible claim amount.
- Detect fraud or inconsistencies.

Incomplete or incorrect documentation may delay claim processing or lead to rejection.

---

# 5.2 Mandatory Documents

The following documents are commonly required:

- Completed Claim Form
- Health Insurance Policy Details
- Hospital Final Bill
- Itemized Bill
- Payment Receipts
- Discharge Summary
- Doctor's Prescription
- Diagnostic Reports
- Pharmacy Bills (if applicable)

Example:

A reimbursement claim is submitted with all mandatory documents.

Result:

The insurer can begin claim assessment.

---

# 5.3 Hospital Documents

The hospital provides several important documents that are required for claim assessment.

Common hospital documents include:

- Admission Record
- Discharge Summary
- Final Hospital Bill
- Itemized Bill
- Investigation Reports
- Operation Notes (if surgery was performed)
- ICU Records (if applicable)
- Doctor's Consultation Notes

These documents help verify:

- Reason for hospitalization
- Diagnosis
- Treatment provided
- Duration of stay
- Total medical expenses

Example:

A patient undergoes appendix surgery.

The hospital submits:

- Admission Record
- Operation Notes
- Discharge Summary
- Final Bill

Result:

The insurer has sufficient medical documents for claim assessment.

# 5.4 Identity and Policy Documents

The insurer also requires documents to verify the identity of the insured person and confirm policy coverage.

Common documents include:

- Health Insurance Card
- Policy Certificate
- Aadhaar Card
- PAN Card (if required)
- Passport (if applicable)

These documents help verify:

- Policy ownership
- Patient identity
- Policy validity

Example:

The patient submits the Health Insurance Card and Aadhaar Card.

Result:

Identity verification is completed successfully.

# 5.5 Additional Supporting Documents

Some claims require additional supporting documents depending on the treatment.

Examples include:

- Referral Letter
- Doctor's Certificate
- FIR (for road accidents)
- Medico-Legal Certificate (MLC)
- Implant Stickers
- Laboratory Reports
- Radiology Reports
- Pharmacy Bills
- Follow-up Consultation Records

These documents help the insurer make an accurate claim decision.

Example:

A patient is hospitalized after a road accident.

Additional documents submitted:

- FIR
- X-ray Report
- Orthopedic Consultation Report

Result:

The insurer can verify that the hospitalization was accident-related.

# 5.6 Missing Document Rules

Before processing a claim, verify that all required documents have been submitted.

Common checks include:

- Claim Form available
- Hospital Final Bill available
- Itemized Bill available
- Discharge Summary available
- Doctor's Prescription available
- Diagnostic Reports available
- Payment Receipts available
- Identity Proof available
- Policy Details available

Decision Guidelines:

- If all mandatory documents are available, continue claim assessment.
- If one or more mandatory documents are missing, request the missing documents before making a final decision.
- If documents appear incomplete or unreadable, request clearer copies.

Example:

Submitted Documents:

- ✔ Hospital Bill
- ✔ Claim Form
- ✘ Discharge Summary

Result:

Request the missing Discharge Summary before processing the claim.

---

# 5.7 Document Verification

Every submitted document should be verified for authenticity and consistency.

Verification includes:

- Patient name matches across all documents.
- Hospital name is consistent.
- Admission and discharge dates are logical.
- Bill amount matches the itemized bill.
- Diagnosis matches the discharge summary.
- Treatment matches the doctor's records.
- Policy details belong to the insured person.

Any mismatch should be reviewed before approving the claim.

Example:

Hospital Bill:
Patient Name – Rahul Sharma

Discharge Summary:
Patient Name – Raj Sharma

Result:

Identity mismatch detected. Additional verification is required.
