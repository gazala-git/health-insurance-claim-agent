# 4.1 What are Policy Exclusions?

Policy exclusions are medical conditions, treatments, services, or situations that are not covered under a health insurance policy.

Even if the policy is active and the waiting period has been completed, a claim may still be rejected if it falls under an exclusion.

Before approving any claim, the insurer must verify whether the treatment is excluded under the policy.

---

# 4.2 Common Exclusions

The following are commonly excluded from health insurance policies:

- Cosmetic or plastic surgery performed only for appearance.
- Treatment related to alcohol or drug abuse.
- Self-inflicted injuries.
- Experimental or unproven treatments.
- Non-medical expenses.
- Illegal or criminal activities.
- Treatments not covered by the policy.

Example:

A patient undergoes cosmetic nose surgery purely to improve appearance.

Result:

The claim is generally not payable because cosmetic procedures are commonly excluded.

---

# 4.3 Cosmetic Treatments

Cosmetic or aesthetic procedures performed only to improve appearance are generally not covered under standard health insurance policies.

Examples include:

- Cosmetic nose reshaping
- Face lift
- Liposuction for cosmetic reasons
- Skin whitening procedures
- Hair transplantation

Exception:

If reconstructive surgery is medically necessary after an accident, burn, or cancer treatment, it may be covered according to the policy.

Example:

A patient undergoes cosmetic rhinoplasty only to improve facial appearance.

Result:

The claim is generally not payable.

---

# 4.4 Self-inflicted Injuries

Medical expenses arising from intentionally self-inflicted injuries are generally excluded from coverage.

Examples include:

- Intentional poisoning
- Self-harm
- Attempted suicide (subject to applicable regulations and policy wording)

Claims are evaluated according to the policy terms and applicable laws.

Example:

Hospitalization due to an intentionally self-inflicted injury.

Result:

The claim may not be payable because it falls under a policy exclusion.

---

# 4.5 Alcohol and Drug Abuse

Treatment primarily resulting from excessive alcohol consumption or abuse of non-prescribed drugs is generally excluded.

Examples include:

- Alcohol intoxication
- Drug overdose due to substance abuse
- Treatment related to illegal narcotic use

Exception:

If the policy specifically provides coverage, the claim will be handled according to those terms.

Example:

A patient is hospitalized due to alcohol intoxication.

Result:

The claim is generally not payable under standard policy exclusions.

---

# 4.6 Experimental Treatments

Experimental, investigational, or unproven medical treatments are generally not covered under standard health insurance policies.

These treatments may not have sufficient clinical evidence or regulatory approval.

Examples include:

- Experimental cancer therapies
- Unapproved stem cell treatments
- Clinical trial procedures not covered by the policy
- Unproven medical devices

Example:

A patient undergoes an experimental therapy that is not approved under the policy.

Result:

The claim is generally not payable.

---

# 4.7 Non-medical Expenses

Health insurance policies usually do not cover non-medical or personal convenience expenses incurred during hospitalization.

Common non-medical expenses include:

- Telephone charges
- Television charges
- Food for visitors
- Extra bed for attendants
- Laundry charges
- Toiletries
- Registration fees (if excluded by the policy)

Example:

Hospital Bill:
₹50,000

Television Charges:
₹500

Visitor Meals:
₹800

Result:

The medical expenses may be payable, but the non-medical expenses are generally not reimbursed.

---

# 4.8 Exclusion Decision Rules

Before approving a claim, verify the following:

- Is the treatment medically necessary?
- Does the treatment fall under any policy exclusion?
- Is the treatment cosmetic?
- Is the hospitalization related to alcohol or drug abuse?
- Is the treatment experimental?
- Are non-medical expenses included in the claim?

Decision Guidelines:

- If no exclusion applies, continue claim assessment.
- If an exclusion applies, the excluded expenses may not be payable according to the policy terms.
- If additional information is required, request supporting medical documents before making a final decision.
